\section{CONCLUSION}
\label{sec:conclusion}
In this paper, we have provided sufficiency and necessity conditions for strategic advantages in both regression and classification settings. These conditions highlight the role of misalignment and the noisy channel parameter $q$ in the receiver's choice between forced and optional revelation modes. Furthermore, we explored the learning problem in both static and dynamic settings, proposing a Bayesian adaptive disclosure protocol for cases with unknown environment parameters and establishing sublinear regret bounds for the learning process. Future work includes the extension to multi-sender environments.
