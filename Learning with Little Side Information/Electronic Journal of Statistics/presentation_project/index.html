01Motivation — University Admissions

🎓 The Scenario

  • A primary university predicts candidates' aptitude from their profiles
  • At test time, a candidate also discloses the secondary university's decision — this is side information
  • How should the primary university leverage this?

⚠️ The Challenge

  • No candidate in the historical data has both admission outcomes simultaneously
  • Training data is partitioned: features + primary labels or features + secondary labels, never both
  • The joint distribution $(X,Y,Z)$ is unlearnable from partitioned data

02Problem Setup

Feature space $\mathcal{X} \subset \mathbb{R}^d$,   Label space $\mathcal{Y} = \{-1,1\}$,   Side info space $\mathcal{Z} = \{-1,1\}$.

Partitioned Dataset

$$D = \{(x_i, y_i)\}_{i=1}^n \;\;\text{(missing } z_i\text{)}, \qquad S = \{(x_j, z_j)\}_{j=n+1}^{n+m} \;\;\text{(missing } y_j\text{)}$$

All samples drawn i.i.d. from the joint $(X,Y,Z)$, but labels and side info are never observed together.

🎯 Goal: At test time, given $(x, z)$, predict label $y$ using both datasets

02Regularity Conditions

C1 · Boundedness

$\exists\;\kappa > 0$ such that $\|X\| \leq \kappa$ a.s.

C2 · Regularity

$X$ and its marginals have densities; CDFs are Lipschitz continuous.

C3 · Positive Definiteness

$\mathbb{E}[(1,X)(1,X)^\top] \succ 0$

C4 · Non-Separability

$\mathbb{P}(Y\!=\!1\mid X\!=\!x, Z\!=\!z) \notin \{0,1\}$ for all $x,z$; similarly for marginals.

Non-separability ensures all optimal weights have finite norm.

03Key Definitions — Three Classifiers

Side-Info Classifier

$\phi_u(x) = 2\,\mathbb{1}_{\{\sigma(u_c + u_x^\top x) > 0.5\}} - 1$,   $u^\star = \arg\min_u L(u)$

Predicts $Z$ from $X$. Trained on $S$.

Primary Classifier

$\psi_v(x) = 2\,\mathbb{1}_{\{\sigma(v_c + v_x^\top x) > 0.5\}} - 1$,   $v^\star = \arg\min_v T(v)$

Predicts $Y$ from $X$ alone (no side info).

Composite Classifier

$\pi_w(x,z) = 2\,\mathbb{1}_{\{\sigma(w_c + w_x^\top x + w_z z) > 0.5\}} - 1$,   $w^\star = \arg\min_w R(w)$

Predicts $Y$ from $(X, Z)$. This is the target.

03Regularized Risks

Regularized logistic population risk:

$$R_\lambda(w) = R(w) + \lambda_c|w_c|^2 + \lambda_x\|w_x\|^2 + \lambda_z|w_z|^2$$

$\phi$-Population Risk Key concept

$$R^\phi(w) = \mathbb{E}_{(X,Y)}\!\Big[\ell\big(X,\phi(X),Y;\,w\big)\Big]$$

Replaces true $Z$ with imputed $\phi(X)$ — this is the risk our algorithm actually optimizes.

Regularization parameter:   $\lambda = \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}},\;\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}},\;\lambda_z\right)$

$\lambda_z$ is the only non-trivial choice — it encodes how much to trust the imputed side information.

04Algorithm — Learning with Side Information

1
Learn $X \to Z$ mapping
Train $\hat{u} = \arg\min_u \hat{L}(u) + \mu\|u\|^2$ on $S$, defining side-info classifier $\hat\phi$.
2
Impute missing side information
For each $(x_i, y_i) \in D$, compute $z_i = \hat\phi(x_i)$.
3
Train composite classifier with regularization
$\hat{w}_\lambda = \arg\min_w \hat{R}^{\hat\phi}(w) + \lambda_c|w_c|^2 + \lambda_x\|w_x\|^2 + \lambda_z|w_z|^2$
4
Predict at test time
Given real $(x, z)$, predict $\hat{y} = \text{sign}(\hat{w}_{c} + \hat{w}_x^\top x + \hat{w}_z z)$.

05Main Result — Upper Bound on Excess Risk

Theorem (Main Theorem)

With probability $\geq 1 - 4\delta$:

$$R(\hat{w}_\lambda) - R(w^\star) \;\leq\; \underbrace{\frac{C_1}{\sqrt{n}}}_{\text{estimation error}} \;+\; \underbrace{\min\!\Big\{\frac{\lambda_z|w_z^\star|^2}{T(v^\star)\!-\!R(w^\star)},\;1\!+\!\frac{\lambda_z|w^\star_{\lambda,z}|^2}{T(v^\star)\!-\!R(w^\star)}\Big\}\!\big(T(v^\star)\!-\!R(w^\star)\big)}_{\text{regularization bias}} \;+\; \underbrace{\min\!\Big\{\frac{\ln 2}{\lambda_z},\;\mathsf{W}\Big\}\ln 4\Big(\frac{C_2}{\sqrt{m}} + L(u^\star)\Big)}_{\text{imputation error}}$$

Term 1

$O(1/\sqrt{n})$
Standard estimation error from primary data.

Term 2

Scales with $\lambda_z$
Captures gain from side info; controlled by $\lambda_z$.

Term 3

Scales with $1/\lambda_z$
Error from imputing $Z$; increases when $\lambda_z$ is small.

05The $\lambda_z$ Trade-Off

$\lambda_z \to 0$ Full trust in side info
⚡ Tension ⚡
$\lambda_z \to \infty$ Ignore side info

Small $\lambda_z$ → More side info

  • Regularization bias ↓ (Term 2 shrinks)
  • Imputation error ↑ (Term 3 grows)
  • Good when: $Z$ is informative and well-imputed

Large $\lambda_z$ → Ignore side info

  • Regularization bias ↑ (Term 2 grows)
  • Imputation error ↓ (Term 3 shrinks)
  • Good when: $Z$ is noisy or poorly imputed

06Bang-Bang Behavior of $\lambda_z^\star$

Regime I — Large Regularizer

If $\frac{\Lambda_z^\star |w_z^\star|^2}{T(v^\star)-R(w^\star)} \geq 1 + \frac{\ln 2}{T(v^\star)-R(w^\star)}$, then $\Lambda_z^\star = \infty$.

$R(\hat{w}_\lambda) - R(w^\star) = \frac{C_1}{\sqrt{n}} + T(v^\star) - R(w^\star)$

Regime II — Small Regularizer

Under complementary conditions, $\Lambda_z^\star = \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}} \to 0$.

$R(\hat{w}_\lambda) - R(w^\star) \leq \frac{C_1}{\sqrt{n}} + \mathsf{W}\ln 4\big(\frac{C_2}{\sqrt{m}} + L(u^\star)\big)$

Key Takeaway

For large $n,m$ and $L(u^\star) \leq 1$:   the optimal $\Lambda_z^\star$ is either $\infty$ or $0$.

Either fully trust or completely ignore the side information — no middle ground!

07Lower Bound — Fundamental Limitation

Theorem (Lower Bound on Excess Risk)

For any learning algorithm $A$, there exists a distribution of $(X,Y,Z)$ such that:

$$\mathbb{P}\!\Big[R\big(A(D,S)\big) - R(w^\star) > 0.19\Big] \;\geq\; \frac{1}{2}$$

Proof Idea

  1. Construct two joint distributions $(X_1,Y_1,Z_1)$ and $(X_2,Y_2,Z_2)$ with identical marginals $(X,Y)$ and $(X,Z)$.
  2. Since partitioned data only reveals marginals, no algorithm can distinguish the two distributions.
  3. Adversarially pick the true distribution → excess risk $\geq 0.19$ w.p. $\geq 1/2$.

Even with infinite data, the partitioned structure imposes an irreducible learning barrier.

08Simulation Results

Setup

  • 100 random distributions over $(X,Y,Z)$
  • Features: Gaussian mixture model
  • $m = 1000$, $n = 4000$, $t = 20000$ test samples
  • $\lambda_z \in \{0.01, 0.1, 1, 10, \ldots, 10^5\}$

Observation

Optimal $\lambda_z$ strongly concentrates at extreme values — confirming the bang-bang behavior predicted by theory.

Conclusion & Takeaways

📊

Partitioned Data Setting

Addressed classification where labels and side information are never jointly observed during training.

🔧

Algorithm with Regularization

Proposed a two-stage imputation algorithm with $\lambda_z$ controlling trust in imputed side information.

📈

Explicit Excess Risk Bounds

Upper bound decomposes into estimation error, regularization bias, and imputation error.

⚡

Bang-Bang Optimality

Optimal $\lambda_z$ is binary: either fully trust or completely ignore side information.

🚧

Fundamental Lower Bound

Excess risk is bounded away from zero for any algorithm — an irreducible barrier.
